Sanur resort day passes

Every 4–5 star day pass in Sanur, compared — entry price, F&B credit, pool & beach access and how to book. Prices checked 19 Jul 2026 · resident-curated, no paid placement.

Sanur's resort day passes range from free entry (pay only for food & drink) to about IDR 700,000 per adult — but most bundle an F&B credit that offsets the entry, so the real spend is lower. The smartest value is Griya Santrian at IDR 500,000, of which IDR 475,000 comes back as a food-and-drink credit — a net ~IDR 25,000 for a beachfront pool day. For zero entry, Byrd House Beach Club (Segara Village) is free — you just spend on F&B. Kids are typically half price and under-6 free.
Book these direct. Unlike Nusa Dua, none of Sanur's resort day passes are sold on Klook or the DayPass app — you reserve them straight with the hotel (WhatsApp, phone or website), usually a day ahead. Prices below are what the hotels quote.

Frequently asked

How much is a resort day pass in Sanur?

From free (Byrd House, pay F&B only) to about IDR 700,000 per adult. Most include a redeemable F&B credit, so the net spend is lower — e.g. Griya Santrian is IDR 500,000 with a IDR 475,000 credit, netting about IDR 25,000.

Are Sanur day passes on Klook or the DayPass app?

No — unlike Nusa Dua, Sanur's resort day passes are booked directly with the hotel by WhatsApp, phone or website, usually a day in advance.

Which Sanur day pass is best for families?

Griya Santrian (beach daybeds + near-free net cost) and Prama Sanur (pool with water slides). Byrd House is free-entry and family-friendly.

Do day passes include beach access, not just the pool?

Most Sanur passes include both, since the resorts sit on the beachfront boardwalk — Griya Santrian, Byrd House and Andaz all include beach access.
