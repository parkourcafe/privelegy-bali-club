Free beach clubs in Bali

Where you can walk in for free — and where 'free entry' quietly means a daybed minimum spend. Prices checked 19 Jul 2026 · resident-curated, no paid placement.

'Free' almost never means a free day in Bali — and it rarely means a free swim. The honest pattern: commercial beach clubs (Potato Head, FINNS, Single Fin, WooBar) let you walk in without a ticket, but any daybed or table carries a minimum spend (roughly IDR 300,000–1,000,000) you redeem against food and drink — so you only pay if you claim a seat, and a bar stool or general beanbag can cost just a drink. Hotel pools are different: most five-star resorts do not let you simply walk in and swim — you book ahead and pay a day-pass or minimum spend. The only ones we could confirm as genuinely free are Segara Village's Byrd House in Sanur (free, no minimum) and The Apurva Kempinski's Reef Beach Club (free entry and free pool — but you now book ahead). Everywhere else, read 'free' as free to enter, pay for a seat.
'Free entry' ≠ free swim. A hotel pool you can walk into and use for nothing is rare — nearly every venue below charges a seat minimum-spend, and hotel pools need a booking. Read every 'free' here as free to enter, pay for a seat, and confirm the current policy with the venue before promising it to a guest — these terms change.

Frequently asked

Which Bali beach clubs are actually free?

Two we could confirm as genuinely free with no minimum: Segara Village's Byrd House in Sanur, and The Apurva Kempinski's Reef Beach Club (free entry and pool — but you now book ahead). The big commercial clubs — Potato Head, FINNS, Single Fin, La Brisa, The Lawn — are free to enter, but any daybed or table carries a minimum spend. Most hotel pools need a booking and a minimum. Always confirm current terms.

Does 'free entry' mean a free day at the beach club?

No. At most clubs the beanbags and daybeds carry a minimum spend that you redeem against food and drink, so you still pay for whatever you order. You can usually stand at the bar or take a general seat for just the price of a drink.

Which free beach club has the best sunset?

Single Fin in Uluwatu (free to enter, seat minimum, packed on Sunday) and The Lawn in Canggu. For a free daytime pool, Kempinski Reef in Nusa Dua.

Is Potato Head free?

Yes for most of the day. There's an IDR 200,000 sunset cover charge from about 16:00–18:30 that's redeemable against food and drink.
