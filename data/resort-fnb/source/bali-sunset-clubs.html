Bali's best sunset clubs, by area

The whole west coast, ranked and explained — with prices, minimum spends and where to book. (Sanur faces east — that's sunrise.) Prices checked 19 Jul 2026 · resident-curated, no paid placement.

Bali's west coast is the sunset coast: Uluwatu's cliffs, Jimbaran's seafood beach, and Seminyak & Canggu's beach clubs. Nearly all are free (or cheap) to enter with a redeemable seat minimum spend — roughly IDR 150,000–400,000 per person at a table, more for a daybed. For the view it's One Eighty's glass cliff-pool (Uluwatu); for atmosphere Single Fin (Uluwatu) and Potato Head (Seminyak); for a beach it's Sundays (Uluwatu) or Jimbaran's seafood sand. Sanur faces east, so it's sunrise, not sunset.
The sunset rule, island-wide. Book a front-row seat or daybed 1–7 days ahead (more for weekends and full moon), arrive by ~16:30, and check each venue's seat minimum spend — it's redeemable on F&B, not an entry fee. Traffic to the Bukit (Uluwatu/Jimbaran) is heavy at sunset — leave early.

Frequently asked

Where is the best sunset in Bali?

The west coast. For the view, One Eighty's glass cliff-pool at The Edge (Uluwatu); for atmosphere, Single Fin (Uluwatu) or Potato Head (Seminyak); for a beach, Sundays Beach Club (Uluwatu) or Jimbaran's seafood sand.

How much do Bali sunset clubs cost?

Most are free or cheap to enter but charge a redeemable seat minimum spend — roughly IDR 150,000–400,000 per person at a table, more for daybeds and cabanas. It's spent on food and drinks, not an entry fee.

Can you watch sunset in Sanur?

Sanur faces east, so it gets sunrise, not sunset — it's the spot for an early-morning beach walk. For sunset, head to the west coast.

Which area is easiest for a first-timer?

Seminyak and Canggu beach clubs are the most walk-up-friendly; Uluwatu and Jimbaran have the most dramatic settings but need more of a plan (traffic, booking ahead).
