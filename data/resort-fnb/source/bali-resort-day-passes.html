You don't need to stay at a Bali resort to use its pool or beach — most sell a day pass, and much of the price often comes back as food-and-drink credit. Passes start around IDR 150,000 (Ayodya in Nusa Dua, Westin in Ubud) and climb to a IDR 1.7M room-included day at Hilton or Bulgari's luxury day in Uluwatu. Below is every district; open a guide for the full priced list. Frequently asked

How much is a Bali resort day pass?

From about IDR 150,000 (Ayodya in Nusa Dua, Westin in Ubud) up to IDR 1,700,000 for Hilton's room-included day. Most mid-range five-star pools sit around IDR 300,000–600,000, much of it redeemable as food and drink.

Which Bali resorts should I book direct instead of Klook?

In Sanur, every resort day pass is direct-only. In Nusa Dua, Ayodya, Merusaka, Nusa Dua Beach Hotel and Hilton also sell only via WhatsApp or their own sites. Booking direct avoids aggregator markup.

Do you earn commission on these day passes?

No. These district guides are coverage — we list passes so you can compare in one place, with no paid placement and no cut of the booking.

Which district has the cheapest passes?

Ubud and Nusa Dua both start at IDR 150,000; Canggu's Vue Beach Club is about IDR 175,000.
