## 1. Current Meta ads requirements/policies (2025–2026)

**Advertising Standards (umbrella policy).** Meta's ad rules live in the Transparency Center: [Introduction to the Advertising Standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/) (accessed 2026-07-19). Travel is **not a restricted/prohibited vertical** — no special authorization category applies to travel-planning content. The policies that bite for a travel offers/perks product:

- **Unacceptable Business Practices** ([transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/fraud-scams/unacceptable-business-practices/](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/fraud-scams/unacceptable-business-practices/), accessed 2026-07-19): ads must not promote offers using deceptive or misleading practices intended to obtain money or personal information. Practical implication for a "free perks/insider guide" angle: landing page must match the ad claim, pricing/terms must be clear, no fake urgency or fabricated reviews. Meta states it may require **additional verification** of advertisers showing suspicious behavior or operating in scam-targeted categories.
- **Fraud, scams and deceptive practices** ([transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/fraud-scams/fraud-scams-deceptive-practices/](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/fraud-scams/fraud-scams-deceptive-practices/), accessed 2026-07-19).

**Lead ads and consent.** Official sources: [Lead Ad Terms, facebook.com/legal/leadgen/tos](https://www.facebook.com/legal/leadgen/tos); [About Lead Ads Terms and security](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/829597887147190); [About Privacy Policies for Lead Ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/1247534515288168) (all accessed 2026-07-19 via search listing). Requirements per the Lead Ad Terms (as quoted in search results): each lead ad must include (i) all legally required disclosures including offer terms (qualification criteria, expiry, redemption limits), (ii) clear disclosure that submitted data is governed by the advertiser's privacy policy, (iii) a **link to a live privacy policy — mandatory before any lead ad runs**. Sensitive data categories (financial, health, government IDs) may not be collected without prior written permission from Meta. A secondary source ([giovanniperilli.com](https://giovanniperilli.com/en/blog/meta-lead-ads-october-2025-the-new-rules-and-what-really-changes/), accessed 2026-07-19) reports the Lead Ad Terms were **last modified 10 October 2025** and that lead data may only be used for the purpose declared in the form (no repurposing to newsletters without fresh consent) — treat the date/detail as secondary-sourced until read on the legal page directly.

**Website-conversion campaigns without an app.** Fully supported and standard: Sales (website conversion location), Leads (website or messaging conversion location), and Traffic objectives all run without any app. Requirements are a Facebook Page + Meta Pixel/CAPI for conversion optimization (see section 2). Official overview: [Get Started with Meta ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/2039891736091051) (accessed 2026-07-19).

**Advantage+ vs manual for non-ecommerce.** Official doc: [About Advantage+ Sales Campaigns](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/1362234537597370) (accessed 2026-07-19 via listing). Secondary reporting ([Foxwell Digital](https://www.foxwelldigital.com/blog/metas-new-ad-campaign-type-what-it-is-why-it-matters-and-how-to-make-it-perform), [bir.ch guide](https://bir.ch/blog/advantage-plus-sales-campaigns-guide), [Jon Loomer](https://www.jonloomer.com/advantage-plus-sales-app-leads-campaigns/), accessed 2026-07-19): during 2025 Meta **merged the separate "Advantage+ Shopping" and manual flows** — every new Sales/App/Leads campaign now starts in a single unified flow with AI optimization on by default and per-lever toggles (audience/budget/placement) rather than a master ASC switch. Meta claims ~9% average lower CPA with the unified format (Meta-attributed figure via secondary sources — treat as vendor claim). Implication for a non-ecommerce lead-style product: you do not "choose ASC vs manual" any more; you choose **Sales (optimizing to a pixel conversion, e.g. trip save)** or **Leads** objective and decide how much Advantage+ automation to leave on. Related travel product: [travel-inventory Advantage+ catalog ads exist](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/173816983244372) (accessed 2026-07-19) but require a hotel/flight/destination catalog — not relevant to a capped test without inventory feeds.

**Partnership ads (creator whitelisting).** Official docs: [About Partnership Ad Permissions](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/442108830566254), also [help/711659943695726](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/711659943695726) and [Partnership Ads Hub account-level permissions](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/526786565561304) (accessed 2026-07-19 via listing). Requirements: advertiser needs **explicit permission from the creator whose handle the ad runs from**; permissions exist at **content level** (per post) or **account level** (boost any content where the brand is tagged/paid-partnership-labelled); creators must have a Professional (Creator/Business) account and comply with Branded Content Policies; permissions are revocable at any time.

## 2. Minimum credibility requirements for a new Page/account

Official docs (all accessed 2026-07-19 via search listing; direct fetch blocked):

- **Facebook Page** — required as the ad identity; at ad level you select the Page (and optional IG account) the ad runs from ([What are the advertising levels in Meta Ads Manager](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/621956575422138)). To advertise a Page you don't own you need admin/editor/advertiser role on it ([Get Started with Meta ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/2039891736091051)).
- **Ad account** — created in Business Manager/Meta Business Suite; requires a valid payment method ([How to Create an Ad Account in Meta Ads Manager](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/407323696966570)).
- **Advertiser verification** — Meta may require identity/business verification for certain advertisers and for ads-transparency ("paid for by") purposes: [Verification Requirements for Advertisers](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/810450577622394); [About advertiser verification for ads transparency](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/983527276402621); [Verify Your Business in Meta Business Suite](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/2058515294227817). Business verification is **not universally required** to start running ads; it is triggered for certain features/categories or suspicious-behavior flags.
- **Domain verification** — required to edit link previews and formerly for iOS-14 event prioritization; confirms site ownership via DNS TXT record, HTML file upload, or meta tag (methods summarized by [Ecwid's help doc](https://support.ecwid.com/hc/en-us/articles/9966096272028-Verifying-domain-to-sell-on-Facebook-and-Instagram-by-Meta), accessed 2026-07-19; the official Business Help article exists in Meta Business Suite > Brand Safety but its exact URL was not directly verifiable this session — [NO DATA] on the canonical URL).
- **Pixel/CAPI** — [Set up and install the Meta Pixel](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/952192354843755); [About Standard and Custom Website Events](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/964258670337005); [Best Practices for Pixel Standard Event Setup](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/2254103654917599). Meta's stated best practice: run **Conversions API alongside the Pixel with redundant event setup** (same events via both, deduplicated). For "trip save" / "WhatsApp opt-in" you would define these as custom conversions or map to standard events (e.g. `Lead`, `CompleteRegistration`).
- **New-account practical constraints** (widely reported, not an official published rule): new ad accounts face conservative spending limits and higher review friction initially — [NO DATA] from official Meta docs on exact new-account spend-limit figures.

## 5. WhatsApp click-to-chat from AU targeting

**Policy constraints:** No Meta policy prohibiting or restricting click-to-WhatsApp (CTWA) ads targeted at Australia was found — **[NO DATA] on any geo-specific restriction**. The requirements are functional, per official docs (accessed 2026-07-19 via listing): a **connected WhatsApp Business account** (app or API/Cloud API) linked to the Facebook Page ([WhatsApp Business required for click-to-WhatsApp ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/372008008225441 — note: listing URL was facebook.com/business/help/372008982225441); [Connect Your Facebook Page to WhatsApp](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/1583303048513172); [WhatsApp Help: create ads](https://faq.whatsapp.com/512723604104492/?cms_platform=web)). CTWA runs under the **Engagement or Leads objective** with WhatsApp as the conversion location. Standard messaging rules apply (24-hour customer-service window; template messages beyond it on the API).

**Market-fit evidence (the real constraint):**

- UK: WhatsApp holds ~88% messaging-app share and ~88% of UK users open it daily ([tyntec WhatsApp statistics](https://www.tyntec.com/blogs/whatsapp-statistics/); [bizibl 2025 messaging roundup](https://bizibl.com/marketing/article/most-popular-messaging-apps-worldwide-2025), accessed 2026-07-19 — public estimates).
- Australia: only ~33% of mobile messaging-app users reported using WhatsApp (Statista, April 2022 survey — dated; [statista.com/statistics/291540](https://www.statista.com/statistics/291540/mobile-internet-user-whatsapp/)); 2025 roundups report **Facebook Messenger leads daily/monthly active usage in AU while WhatsApp leads only downloads** ([bizibl](https://bizibl.com/marketing/article/most-popular-messaging-apps-worldwide-2025)). Current AU WhatsApp user counts sit behind Statista/eMarketer paywalls — **[NO DATA]** on a precise 2025-26 AU penetration figure.
- Planning implication (analyst judgment, not sourced fact): for an AU-primary audience, WhatsApp opt-in as the *sole* conversion action risks suppressing conversion volume vs a UK audience; keep "trip save" (pixel event) as the primary optimization event and treat WhatsApp opt-in as a secondary, higher-intent action.
