# Other Bali — Question-Engine Marketing Content Pack

Ready-to-post copy built from the 100-question tourist map and the GM-proxy
answers. Every piece drives to a **real, live Other Bali page** so the marketing
and the site reinforce each other.

**Voice rules (same as the site):** honest and fit-context, never hype. No
"#1 / top 10", no invented facts, no negative call-outs about specific places.
English for anything public. Prices as ranges, never exact.

## 3 · WhatsApp concierge answers

Copy-paste replies for guest questions. Warm, short, one clear link. Sign off
as **Fadjri, Other Bali**. Swap `[area]` for the guest's district.

### W1 — "First day in Bali?"
> Welcome! 🌴 Keep day one easy — check in, eat near your villa, get a massage, and catch sunset only if you still have energy. Don't book a far tour today; you'll just sit in traffic. Full first-day plan here: otherbali.com/first-time-in-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W2 — "What do we do if it rains?"
> No stress — Bali rain usually passes in waves. Make it a soft day: a spa, a long lunch, a café, or a cooking class close to you, and we move waterfalls/sunset to a clearer day. Ideas by area: otherbali.com/bali-rainy-day — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W3 — "Beach club recommendation?"
> Happy to help — quick question: couple, group, or with kids? The right one really depends on that (and on timing — 2pm and 8pm feel like different places). Meanwhile, the by-area list: otherbali.com/best-beach-clubs-in-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W4 — "One day in Ubud — what should we do?"
> Go early and keep it calm: Monkey Forest or the rice terraces in the morning, lunch in the centre, a spa, then an early dinner. Skip the far waterfall marathon unless you've got a driver. Route here: otherbali.com/ubud-one-day — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W5 — "We're not renting a scooter."
> Totally fine — stay local and use Grab/Gojek for short hops; for day trips we'll sort a driver. Just depends a bit on your base. How it works: otherbali.com/how-to-get-around-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W6 — "Where should we eat tonight, near us?"
> For tonight I'd keep it close and easy rather than crossing town in traffic. Tell me your area and group size and I'll point you to a couple near you. Our by-area picks: otherbali.com/best-restaurants-in-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W7 — "Best sunset spot?"
> Sunset is a west/south-coast thing here — Uluwatu cliffs are the most dramatic, Canggu/Seminyak have the beach clubs. Arrive early and pair it with dinner nearby so you're not driving in the dark. Spots: otherbali.com/where-to-watch-sunset-in-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W8 — "Family half-day idea?"
> Keep it short and sweet: breakfast, one main activity (beach, pool, rice fields or a workshop), early lunch, then rest. That's plenty. More: otherbali.com/bali-with-kids — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W9 — "We want to try local food — where's safe?"
> Great call — go for a busy warung with fresh turnover. Start with nasi campur, satay lilit or grilled fish, and go mild if you're spice-sensitive. Our picks: otherbali.com/best-warungs-in-bali — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W10 — "Should we do a driver for the day?"
> Yes for anything with several stops — Ubud, waterfalls, Uluwatu sunset. It's low-stress and you actually enjoy the day. I can arrange one — just tell me the date and roughly what you'd like to see. — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W11 — "Canggu or Ubud for the rest of our trip?"
> Depends on the mood you want: Canggu for surf/cafés/beach clubs, Ubud for rice fields and calm. If you've got more than 5 nights, do a few of each. Honest comparison: otherbali.com/ubud-vs-canggu — Fadjri, Other Bali

### W12 — "Plan our whole day?"
> Love that — here's a ready morning-to-night shape you can follow or tweak: otherbali.com/my-day. Tell me your area and I'll tailor it. — Fadjri, Other Bali
