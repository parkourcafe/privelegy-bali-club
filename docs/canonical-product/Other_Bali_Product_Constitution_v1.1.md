## 1. Core Idea

Other Bali helps people choose the right place for the moment they are in.

The product is not simply a list of places, a discount app, or a travel blog. It is a resident-curated decision guide for Bali: a way for travellers and residents to understand where to go based on their current situation, mood, company, appetite, budget, and practical needs.

The central question Other Bali answers is not:

> What are the best places in Bali?

It answers:

> Where should I go right now, for the kind of moment I am in?

## 2. Positioning

**Other Bali is a resident-curated decision guide for Bali.**

It helps travellers choose the right place for the moment they are in — a quiet work morning, first dinner after landing, sunset with friends, a family day in Sanur, a date night in Uluwatu, or a slow Sunday in Ubud.

## 7. Recommendation Philosophy

Every recommendation in Other Bali should answer these questions:

1. What job does this place solve?
2. Who is it good for?
3. When is it good?
4. When is it not good?
5. What should the traveller order or do there?
6. What should they maybe skip?
7. What practical constraints matter?
8. Why does a resident, curator, or editor trust it?

Other Bali does not rank places only by stars, popularity, trendiness, or paid promotion.

A place belongs in Other Bali because it fits a real moment.

## 12. Dish Recommendations

Other Bali should eventually recommend dishes personally, not generically.

The product should avoid giving meat recommendations to a vegetarian user, or party recommendations to a quiet-family user.

Dish recommendation layers:

- most recommended overall;
- vegetarian picks;
- vegan picks;
- good for kids;
- good to share;
- date-night order;
- healthy/light order;
- local food order;
- dessert/drink picks;
- maybe skip / mixed feedback.

Important language rule:

Do not use harsh public wording like:

> Worst dishes.

Use:

- Maybe skip;
- Less consistent;
- Mixed feedback;
- Not the reason to come.

Important sourcing rule:

Other Bali must not scrape or republish Google reviews. Dish recommendations should come from:

- resident/curator notes;
- partner-provided recommendations;
- Other Bali guest feedback;
- editorial visits;
- manual menu research without copying third-party review text.

## 17. Money Model

The tourist does not pay.

Other Bali should not monetize through tourist-side payments in the early product.

Canonical commercial direction:

- organic ranking cannot be bought;
- paid/sponsored visibility must be labeled separately;
- venues/services pay only when there is real value generated;
- for restaurants, the cleanest model is confirmed seated reservations through a booking partner such as TablePilot;
- QR/perk redemption is proof/supporting signal, not the billing event;
- perks are optional and should not define the brand.

Current money model statement:

> Other Bali is free for travellers. Venues cannot buy organic ranking. We earn only when we help create real, attributable value such as confirmed seated visits or clearly labeled commercial campaigns.

## 18. Perks

Perks are useful, but they are not the identity of the product.

Correct framing:

- resident pick;
- perk available;
- simple benefit;
- show screen at venue;
- optional bonus.

Incorrect framing:

- coupon app;
- discount club;
- privilege card;
- endless deals;
- fake exclusivity.

Public UI should not show internal wording such as:

- proposed perk;
- pending partner negotiation;
- draft benefit.

If a perk is not confirmed, either hide it or phrase it in a tourist-safe way.

## 19. Booking

Other Bali should not build its own booking engine in the early product.

Architecture:

```txt
Other Bali discovery → Reserve → TablePilot / venue booking system → confirmed seated visit → aggregate report → venue fee
```

Other Bali should track:

- reservation click;
- source;
- venue;
- booking partner result when available;
- confirmed seated/billable count from partner reports.

## 20. Feedback, Credits, Cashback

User feedback is strategically important.

Ideal future loop:

1. user discovers place through Other Bali;
2. user books or visits;
3. user leaves structured feedback;
4. feedback improves recommendations;
5. user earns credits or benefits;
6. credits can be used for selected perks/experiences.

Important constraint:

Do not launch real cashback before legal/payment review.

Cashback creates complexity around:

- payments;
- wallet balances;
- fraud;
- refunds;
- tax;
- Google Play policy;
- consumer protection;
- possible financial-services rules.

Safer phased language:

> Earn Other Bali Credits after confirmed visits and useful feedback.

Credits should initially be non-cash, non-withdrawable, and usable only inside the product.

## 29. Canonical Platform Boundary: Decision → Action → Navigation

This boundary is fixed.

### Other Bali owns the decision

Other Bali is responsible for:

- understanding the user’s moment and constraints;
- selecting and explaining the right places or services;
- resident/editorial judgment;
- `Why it fits`, `Best for`, `Not for`, and practical intelligence;
- menus and structured offering information where verified;
- `What to order` and other editorial recommendations;
- moment pages, routes, lists, and experience scenarios;
- the action interface;
- source attribution and aggregate partner reporting;
- Saved and My Bali;
- trust, freshness, and disclosure rules.

### Partner systems own fulfilment

Venues and specialist providers remain responsible for:

- real-time table inventory;
- reservation confirmation, changes, cancellation, and no-show handling;
- menu availability and fulfilment;
- kitchen operations;
- delivery coverage, drivers, timing, and fees;
- payments, refunds, disputes, and customer support;
- actual delivery of the hospitality or lifestyle service.

Other Bali may make these actions feel coherent inside one product, but it does not pretend to own operations it cannot reliably control.

### Google Maps owns navigation

Google Maps is the navigation infrastructure, not a product Other Bali should reproduce.

Google Maps remains responsible for:

- turn-by-turn navigation;
- live traffic;
- route calculation;
- ETA;
- road conditions and closures;
- global place coverage;
- navigation-mode choice.

Other Bali is responsible for:

- deciding which place fits;
- explaining why it fits;
- showing the relevant practical context;
- handing the user to Google Maps through `Open in Google Maps`.

### Canonical product sentence

> Other Bali helps you decide where to go and why. Google Maps gets you there.

### Canonical user flow

```txt
Moment / need
→ Other Bali recommendation
→ Why it fits
→ Menu / practical details
→ Reserve | Order | Takeaway | Open in Google Maps | Save
→ Trusted external system fulfils the action
→ Other Bali records attribution where permitted
```

### Google Maps integration contract

Each place that supports navigation should store or resolve:

```txt
provider
google_place_id where available
latitude
longitude
formatted_address
google_maps_deep_link
navigation_verified_at
```

Rules:

- use Google Maps as a handoff, not as the editorial brain;
- do not scrape or republish Google Maps review text;
- do not make Google rating the primary ranking logic;
- do not build proprietary turn-by-turn navigation;
- do not promise live traffic or ETA unless supplied by the active maps provider;
- keep venue coordinates and navigation links verified;
- track the outbound navigation action only within privacy rules.

### Explicit non-goals

Other Bali will not build:

- a Google Maps clone;
- a global place database designed to out-cover Google;
- turn-by-turn navigation;
- a live traffic engine;
- a road-routing engine;
- a copied Google review corpus.

```txt
Moments are the product entry point.
Categories remain the data structure, not the main user mental model.

Menus — yes.
Editorial “What to order” — yes.
Dish discovery — yes, when curated and decision-useful; not automatic thin SEO pages.

Reservations — yes, through a gateway/handoff.
Other Bali-owned booking engine — no.

Delivery — yes, through direct restaurant or delivery-provider handoffs.
Other Bali-owned courier network — no.

Takeaway — yes.
Pre-order — only as a partner-confirmed capability.

Google Maps — yes, as navigation infrastructure.
Other Bali-owned navigation engine — no.

My Bali / Saved — yes.
Perks — optional supporting layer, not brand identity.
Tourist-side payment product — no by default.
Wallet / real-money cashback — not a base product.
AI concierge — later only as an interface over verified structured data.
“Travel Operating System” — internal ambition at most, not public positioning.
```

```txt
decision · explanation · trusted action interface · attribution · relationship
```
