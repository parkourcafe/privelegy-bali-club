# Other Bali data inventory

Status: engineering inventory as of 2026-07-14. This is not a final privacy notice, legal-basis decision, retention schedule, data-processing agreement register or App Store privacy-label submission.

## Governance blockers

- **OWNER/LEGAL BLOCKER — controller identity:** the repository exposes `support@otherbali.com`, but does not establish the controller's legal entity name, registered address or privacy representative.
- **LEGAL BLOCKER — lawful bases and retention:** explicit choices/checkboxes are recorded where noted, but counsel/owner has not approved the legal-basis mapping or maximum retention periods. The venue-photo crash reconciler removes only grace-aged unconsented failed work; it is not a retention policy. No approved scheduled retention job is present for the rows marked unknown below.
- **RELEASE BLOCKER — App Store declarations:** the App Store categories below are engineering candidates only. `PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy` provisionally declares Preferences/UserDefaults reason `CA92.1` with tracking, collected-data types and tracking domains empty; binary/dependency accessed-API scanning and App Store questionnaire review are still required.
- **SCOPE NOTE:** the first iOS shell contains public guide/cache features and no GA SDK/tag. Web partner onboarding and guide-lead forms are controller data flows but are not exposed as native first-build screens. Their relevance to App Store labels must still be confirmed against Apple's current rules.
- **PROCESSOR BLOCKER:** final Vercel/Supabase regions, subprocessors, infrastructure-log fields and retention are not configured or evidenced in this repository. Confirm them in the live provider accounts and DPAs.
- **ABUSE-CONTROL BLOCKER:** HMAC proof, exact-Origin checks and per-GuestRef limits do not bound repeated fresh cookie jars. Configure and evidence edge/WAF global quotas and bot controls before promoting write-heavy or guide-lead surfaces.

“Linked” below means linked in the application schema to a GuestRef, contact or submission—not necessarily Apple's final definition. “Tracking” means cross-company/cross-app tracking; first-party product analytics is listed as not tracking unless a future configuration changes that fact.

## First-build conclusions

- The native shell stores only public guide/cache and local saved/navigation state. It has no account, ad SDK, GA tag or cross-app tracking code.
- Google Analytics is disabled by default and for the first build; the conditional web implementation must not be enabled until processor, retention, notice and App Store/web consent reviews are updated.
- The current export/delete endpoints cover only data linked to the current GuestRef. They do **not** cover guide leads, venue representative/photo evidence, provider logs/backups or data sent to external destinations.
- All pre-`0041` photo submissions require exact production Storage/digest/consent reconciliation. The observed 91 live draft-path images and 608 private official-source candidates do not have publication rights merely because they are reachable or collected.
- A guide-lead request sent from an arbitrary address does not by itself prove control of the stored email/WhatsApp contact. No guide-lead data may be disclosed or deleted until the manual verification procedure is staffed and evidenced.
- A successful GuestRef deletion removes consent/redemption/save/list identity rows and dish-feedback user content, then unlinks bounded usage events. A raw-ref-free deletion tombstone deliberately remains as a resurrection barrier; its approved retention is still open. Deletion does not promise immediate erasure from provider backups or establish a retention period for anonymous aggregates.
- App Store labels and the privacy manifest remain open release evidence, not completed artifacts.
